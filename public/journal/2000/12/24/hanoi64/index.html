Hanoi64

By Rob Nugen

Tower of Hanoi with 64 rings

12:03am CST Sunday 24 December 2000

I have just started emacs on the tower of hanoi puzzle with sixty-four rings. It just got the sixth ring off the big stack.

https://writing.colostate.edu/assignments/archives/me514/lab2.htm

Here is an applet that solves the puzzle, so you can see what I'm talking about. Please note that while the rings on this java applet instantly move from one stack to another, the rings on the lovely emacs version actually move up from the stack, across the screen and down onto the next stack.

12:10am

emacs is working to get the ninth ring off. I'm going to bed.

08:41am

emacs is working to get the sixteenth ring off. It has clearly made it further than it had last night when I stopped the previous process. It seems this will take a really long time.

09:16am

emacs is still working on the sixteenth ring. I think it will get it before I leave for YRUU meeting today.

I'm just about to write letters or something on my newly clean blue desk thing.

11:01am

I just wrote a few postcards to some of my peeps and filed some stuff in a scrap book.

Assuming emacs moves one ring per second, I think I just calculated correctly that emacs will finish this tower of hanoi in 5 * 10^11 years. Yup.

emacs is still working to uncover the sixteenth ring. I'm leaving now until tomorrow afternoon.
