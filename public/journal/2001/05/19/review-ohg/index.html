review ohG

By Rob Nugen

MK Ultra

Artist: ohGr label: Spitfire Records

Title: weLt

Rob Nugen #75

19 May 2001

//+, //, //-, /+, /, - rec: playlist

from the flyer:

o-gre (oh grr)

[French probably ultimately from Latin Orcus,god of the underworld.]

1. A giant or monster in legends and fairy tales that eats human beings.

2. A person who is felt to be particularly cruel, brutish, or hideous.

3. Former lead singer of Skinny Puppy

 

I keep imagining a gentle version of Marilyn Manson as I listen to this disc. Or electronic pop in the underworld. Some of the lyrics try to be scary, but they're not.

 

The music reaches into the brain for a foothold, using multiple personalities in each song to keep it interesting. I believe this is love it or hate it music.

 

I'm rating each of these as //(+).

 

1. 4:27 Water grinding penetrating. soothing outro

2. 4:37 Devil funk beat, buzzy music, barely tweaked vox

3. 3:50 Kettle buzzy tones pounded into music

4. 3:21 Earthworm electronic upright bass meets british electronic pop.

5. 3:50 Lusid depeche mode on speed

6. 3:57 Pore Manic panic beats. How does he talk so fast?

7. 4:08 Chaos deep slow organ, fuzzy goth

8. after 10 3:31 Cracker Fast pace, yet slow lyrics and bass

9. 4:47 Solow layers of tweaked bubbly beats and buzzy rock and vox

10. 3:16 Suhleap P.I.L. meets old Front 242 maybe

11. 4:19 Minus plodding massive beats
