espanol

By Rob Nugen

10:56pm Thursday 17 May 2001

I'm being lazy and cutting and pasting part of what I wrote to Sally in an email into my journal. Good random stuff. In two languages.

Aprendando español es bien, pero no estudio muy mucho. Estudio a veces. (Is aprender "to learn"? Is aprendando "learning"? Can I say "learning spanish is good" that way?)

Ayer, hablo con mi viejo amigo Francisco, quien hable español all his life (todo él life ? (todo él viv-something?)). Dice que tengo que aprender uno palabra cada dia. Hablando con amigos en español es muy fun, y Luz Clarita (Wende's name en clase de español) y yo might comemos con Dude y Veronica, y hablamos en español.

I'm guessing the sentence structure at the end of my paragraph is incorrect. Am I trying to use the future imperfect? I have no idea what is the name of the type of speech I'm trying to write. I know we haven't learned it yet.

Dude and Veronica saben español muy bien, y son even mas buenos amigos que Francisco.

- - - -

Wende and I got our internet access re-established after 4 weeks of no access at home! Today we had cable modem connected, and it's a pretty refreshing thing to have back. And, as a side benefit, I can watch The Simpsons and Futurama on Fox with a clear picture now.

The guy had to give me a cable splitter though; we were not listed to get TV access when he hooked up our cable internet access. And, I still have to actually terminate the ends of the cables to hook up the TV. The splitter is a great start though. :-)
