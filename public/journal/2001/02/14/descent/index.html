14 Feb 2001, 00:00

Descent

By Rob Nugen

7:08 am

Breakfast. Sausage and eggs or banana cinamon crepes with coconut.

Wende had sausage because she didn't want coconut. I had crepes because mmmmmm banana cinamon.

We are just west of Noumea now.

3 hours to go.

513 mph 
38000 feet 
-59 F = -51 C

I think our steward looks like Keanu Reeves, but Wende says, "no, he (steward) is cute."

9:09 am

Just watched Bedazzled. That's a movie that Wende and I enjoyed at the theater.

507mph
38000 feet
-58F

the video says we are very close to Sydney, and therefore very close to land, and suddenly there is terrific cloudcover; is it due to the continent?

Will I be able to see the shore? Oh - we aren't over land yet.. maybe 20 minutes?

So the water has been mesmerizing to me. Texture like an orange peel closeup. There was a portion where I could see the orange peel dots and then gentle waves way larger than the dots, not affecting the dots per se. Very interesting.

The clouds we're over look like cirrus clouds, and a layer of cumulus under them. I can't really see the water, but there are patches of non-clouds, but they don't line up for me.

9:20 am

I can see water again. Oh, and there seems to be a oh my gosh we are right over the coast!!!

Like a perfect fractal from blue to tan to rolling green hills and a city. I wonder where we are. The screen says Wollongong. Hmm.

Now it's all clouds again. I'm thankful to have gotten to see the shore!

So I was saying that I saw a lot of little white dots on the water, and many of them were miles and miles out, yet still all clusterd; not sparse.. Could they have been boats?

500 mph
28 minutes left. 
still at 38000 feet.

but I just felt a tilt. It didn't seem like a downward tilt, but a turning tilt.

I can see on the map our tilt right that I felt a while ago.

497 mph.
-50 C
25 minutes left. 
make that 24
38000 feet still.

We have traveled 7320 miles so far.

9:36 am

487 mph now.

9:57 am

Just now began our descent.

I'm turning off now; I assume they will ask us to in a second.
