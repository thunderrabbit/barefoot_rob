Takeoff

By Rob Nugen

13 February 2001

 6:32 pm Melbourne
11:32 pm Los Angeles
 1:32 am Houston
7935  miles to go
12771 kilometers to go

On the 747 watching huge blast from the engien. From this seat I can see the back of the outer left engine and the water on the ground being blown hard backward.

We are taxiing around the airport. I have Wende's watch so I can fill in the time needed to accelerate to takeoff. I wonder what airspeed that is.

They have given us headphones and socks and eyemask and pillow and blanket.

Chillin beside sign [[Q1] <- Q -> ] at 1:43am CST. We're 30 minutes late taking off.

11:46 pm PST

Moving now from Q1 sign.

11:48 pm PST

We are stopped again.

11:51 pm

Still stopped.

Wow suddenly I'm crazy tired.

11:52 pm

Engines winding up we are moving. Taxiing [T 25R-7L]

We are en queue for takeoff but still need to turn around.

11:54 pm

huge aleron on wing. Extended way back. Barely taxiing about to turn around I think. Since we got on 25 R-7L runway 2 planes have taken off before us. One just now (737) and one 747 and another 737. Three total.

11:58 pm

I just heard a tone and the captain said gently, "sit down!" A 747 is rolling beside us and bang they go. We are rumbling behind and beside them and now turning around on U7L. I can see 4 planes in queue behind us.

We are 90 degrees, 135, 170, 180 degrees turned.

12:00am local time 7pm in Melbourne.

We are just about to GO!

I have Wende's second hand on her watch.
