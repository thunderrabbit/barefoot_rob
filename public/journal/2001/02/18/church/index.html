Church

By Rob Nugen

11:39 am Sunday 18 February 2001

Beaner and I took the train to Jollimont and walked by teh botanical gardens to the Melbourne Unitarian Peace Memorial Church.

Small gongregation in this room that could seat 200 comfortably, though it would be hot.

Today there are two speakers who described the situation in Indonesia with East Timor becoming free and how to help facilitate the process so the residents can actually have soveriegnty and make decisions about things that actually affect their lives.

Now we are in questions and answer session.

19 people in the room here includng the young adult speakers and Bryn and I. about 10 people over age 60.

Bryn's nose is experiencing allergies and sniffles.

The members here seem much more aware of the situation than I am.

12:39pm

(this is being written on Thursday 22 Feb)

After the fellowship, Bryn and I joined them for sandwiches and conversation. We talked to a man named Bob who had been a professional lacrosse player and is now in charge of lacrosse stuff or something. He was part of the over 60 crowd at the church.

He described getting in trouble for unnecessary roughness at a game. Bryn and I wondered later about his grandkids all, "Grandpa, you're the coolest grandpa ever!" while he sat in the penalty box.
