Dream

By Rob Nugen

7:01am CST Sunday 7 January 2001

(dream)

I was inside the Chevron at the corner trying to buy gas or something, and a woman with nothing better to do was hanging out with the cashier all day. In his drawer he had some donuts along with the money, and she casually reached over and grabbed a donut to eat. The cashier said, don't grab anything out of the drawer," explaining about what if she stole money that wold be bad, etc.

She agreed that would be bad and said she wouldn't steal any.

next moment, she was reaching over and took out two quarters. Just at that moment, he saw her so she did a magic trick sleight of hand and made the quarters seem to disappear from her hands.

"C'mon, I told you not to steal anything..."

"c'mon, just try to guess what hand they are in.." she begged.

I casually picked up the phone and dialed 9911, but it didn't connect. I asked if I could use the phone, and how to dial out.

"sure, just dial straight out."

I dialed 911 and walked outside with the long stretchy phone cord so the woman wouldn't hear me and freak.

The dispatcher came on the line "Fire, Ambul.."

I cut her off, "pleece."

"Please?"

"no, Police"

I should have let her finish her question before I spoke in a rush.

"I'll connect you."

A little girl ran up from the parking lot.

"Robert, do you want to play tag with us?"

"In a second."

"Well, you're IT when you want to play."

"Okay."

I was till on hold, and I noticed they were watching me from inside, waiting for the phone. They were trying to close the store.

I hung up on the hold music and went inside and told them what I had been doing.

"now the cops are going to just show up because they have the caller ID and will just drive up to see what's going on."

Hmmm.. certainly a dilemma.

Well, I'll be here for a while playing tag with the youth; if they show up in that amount of time, I'll explain what happened."

Turns out it didn't matter; the police drove up just at that moment. I bounded outside to greet them.

The officer looked a bit startled, so I put my hands in plain view. I started to speak, but it looked like they were on the radio dispatch.

"Can you listen now?"

"Yes."

Okay. It's over now, but there was a woman inside with nothing better to do, and I witnessed her stealing two quarters out of the drawer. I realize it's only two quarters, but that's the point, or it's not the point, or something is the point; she shouldn't steal."

"Yeah, like if she stole your key ring, you'd quit being a customer there and they'd lose a customer," the cop offered by example of why she shouldn't steal even small things.

"Yeah," I agreed, not knowing how her example applied.

and I woke up.
