10 Jun 2001, 00:00

OKC sunday am

By Rob Nugen

8:21 am Sunday 10 June 2001

I feel exceptiionally blessed that I have been allowed to sleep through cleanup. I wonder if it's unfair to the community, or ultiately in the greater good because they can all sleep on the way home, and I will likely have to drive. (Please note: I did clear with Susie and Denise that they would drive home first, and I would sleep the first few hours, giving me a total of about 6 hours of sleep before driving the remaining 4 hours home.)

Allison is climbing out of a tree. There is yelling outside. Announcing closing circle. I'm going to pack my stuff and go.

8:39 am

I was just asked by Scottie et. al. to do Aristasha (long version). We had plenty of time so I didn't have to do Aristasha (short version). I wonder if we can make up a workable Aristasha (extended remix), or maybe Aristasha (dance mix) haha okay, I'm taking this too far.

But the reason I'm writing is that Katy R is funny. Advertising SWUUSI:

Elizabeth: "What is SWUUSI??"

Katy: "It's week! It's a rally! It's a week long rally!"

Heheeheee that was funny.

You have given me a treasure. I love you so.

10:57 am

We ate at IHOP; I left a pretty big tip because we pretty much inundated the waitstaff, and our waitress was cute cute.

Hugs and hugs and hugs and hugs many times over, several tears from some youth and hopefully supportive words from me.

1:43 pm

During the trip so far, Gram and Brian have been talking only about computer security. I pretty much haven't been able to follow any of their conversation, even if I had been fully awake.

We just stopped for munchies and wizzies. I got ice cream and cookies. Ryan flirted with the Subway Sandwiches Girl. Pat got fluorescent green sour squirt candy. Hethre got attacked by a fly on her (Hethre's) way to get ice cream. Cassie and Hethre complained about computer science class (an indirect tangent conversation of Gram and Brian's) in which they are learning C++ as their first language! Allison said she had already waited the 30 minutes. Betsy chose between chocolate and pistachios. Tanner got his snack for $1.86. Christina slept in the car. Other people did other things. We all marveled to hear that Houston had received thirty five inches of rain.

1:58 pm

We just drove by an auto dealership with four cars out front, each with their hood up, and three of which each had a large letter. They apparently are selling ALE. Nice.

6pm

Back at First Church. So amazing to see the water lines where water lines shouldn't be. After we clean out the van, I am going to go ride my bike around and see what I can see.
