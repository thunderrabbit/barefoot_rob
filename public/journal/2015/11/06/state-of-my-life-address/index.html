State of my Life Address

By Rob Nugen

18:59 Friday 06 November 2015 JST

  1. As I write this, I’m going the long way home around the Yamanote Line; I just sat down at Shinjuku station, and I plan to switch to the Keihin Tohoku Line in Ueno or somewhere convenient.

  2. Have had a few tickets stacking up for AB and CB websites, but on 1 November, I released version 2.0.0 of both the frontend and backend of the AB website. It uses a new system for logins which allows the login to be decoubpled from the persona using the site. Soon, different “types” of users will be able to log in.

  3. CB site has requested a few changes as part of their low-key redesign. Basically they want to redo the entire site, but I don’t know how to design well enough to do it, so we’re doing it kinda piecemeal. 16 November will be the next visible change.

  4. Lin and I have started thinking about buying land “out in the country.” I’m happy to do it just as an investment; I imagine its value will appreciate more quickly than if the money just rots in the bank. Lin has a bit more practical aspirations, so somewhere in between we’ll probably get a house that’s not too far out and can be “fixed up” in a reasonable amount of time, whatever that means.

  5. I drew a vision board-ish thing showing the land we’d like to buy, featuring a stream and some adjacent open land on which MKP trainings can be held.

  6. I’ve been in touch with a guy in the US who’s starting an import business, so in a way I’m starting an export business. No one has profited yet except the post office and convenience store where I paid for shipping within Japan, but it’s still a fun project for now.

  7. I’ve not been studying Japanese recently, but I sorta kinda have considered possibly starting again.

  8. I’ve not been drawing mazes recently, and I haven’t really considered starting again as much as I’ve thought about studying Japanese again.

  9. I’ve started drawing images again. It takes a bit of effort to create all the meta data: posting them to my wiki, my URL shortener, and Patreon, but it’s still pretty fun.

  10. I recently bought some new markers, but haven’t used them yet! Hopefully I will like the colors.

  11. I sent Fred’s 2016 journal today. He likes that late nights and weekend days get ample space for entries.

  12. I may start teaching lessons on Friday night; it’s for a single semester if in fact they decide to start. Trial lesson is next week or something.

  13. Ted has started coming back into the house, but he hasn’t yet stayed inside with the door closed. He regularly comes in to eat, snoop around the house a bit, and go back out. We could close the door while he’s in snooping mode, but I want him to feel comfortable that he can go back out.

  14. Lin has been working lots at work, and I often don’t see her until 10pm at night. She enjoys the work, using English while helping her clients, and getting to see lots of different houses.

  15. We recently bought a Dyson vacuum cleaner, which is battery operated, and lighter than our previous plug-in version. It’s pretty neat, and has their signature clear dust catcher in which all the dander has a party before heading on a new adventure.

  16. I’m planning to give Lin a massage coupon which says something like 2016 Special Massage Coupon, and has a checkbox by each month of the year, plus an extra checkbox in March for her birthday, and July for our anniversary. Don’t tell her.

  17. Also planning to get her Harrod’s Bears for 2011 and 2015, to bookend the three she has already. Don’t tell her.

  18. Pop Pop Chaos features variable pricing for bubbles! There are about 600 blue bubbles now, and they cost about 700 points to make new ones. There are approximately 0 green bubbles now, and whoever logs in can gain points for creating new ones.

    I’ve kinda mussed up user thunderrabbit on the game though; I hyper-clicked to create blue bubbles and laughed as Meteor didn’t keep up with my clicks, allowing me to go past my point credit limit. Then it caught up and I’m in the red like 75000 points. Dang.

  19. My plan to meet Lin tonight worked! I’ve just switched to Keihin-Tohoku line in Ueno (by simply walking across the platform), chose one of the plentiful seats, and will arrive in Kawasaki around the same time as Lin so we can have a nice date!

  20. Tariq and I have been working on an app for Apple TV; it’s sorta kinda almost finished, though I still need to work out a couple of bits that I’m not sure how to do. It basically presents 140 business vocabulary words which are the most commonly used + most commonly unknown by his students. Tariq has worked for 10 years compiling the list, so it’s certainly valuable!

  21. Tokyo I-group will have its first open meeting on Monday November 16th in Hiroo from 6pm - 8pm. One of my mates from Japan will join us; I’m optimistic he will benefit from the work and attend regularly.

  22. This coming Saturday Lin and I will be going to a Reiki share in Asakusa. Oh maybe while we’re in the area we can get some good prezzies for people in the US for belated Christmas.

  23. While in Texas, Lin and I plan to go skydiving, eat steak, and get officially married in the US! Might even get a driver’s license for me, cause they cost $3000 in Japan.

  24. Haha I just realized this is the second time I’ve been in Hamamatsucho today. First time was for a lesson, and I left on Yamanote Line; this time is just passing through on Keihin Tohoku Line.

  25. At the toy store I visit each week before MM lesson, I found an intricate maze puzzle had been broken with a bit of brute force, probably applied by an over enthusiastic person. The store employees were like, “welp, you can have it!” and I was like, “yay!” cause I fixed it in about an hour (not counting time to let the glue dry), and it’s perfect now. Retail price 5800 yen or so.

  26. I should show the maze to Travis before taking it to the US. It’s basically the same architecture as he suggested once, but with one extra, um, twist.

  27. Speaking of Travis, I left my neobbbbium magnets at his house a couple weeks ago when we went for a visit to see them and Johan, Matt, and Claire. Maybe we can arrange for a swap; I’ll show him the maze (before taking it to Fred in the US) in exchange for the magnets. (not that they’re being held hostage)

  28. In Minecraft, I /sethome in a cave under a lava-soaked mountain. I immediately dug a staircase down to level 10 or so, and have been digging a “huge” hole around the staircase, just because it’s funny. Man, what a waste of time.

    I need to somehow convince myself to spend as much time drawing in real life as I do digging in Minecraft.

  29. Lin and I have been eating much(!) more healthily for … a week now.

  30. For the past three weeks, I have joined an online I-group on Thursday mornings, Tokyo time. I guess at a basic level it amounts to life coaching and holding each other accountable for achieving our individual personal goals.

  31. Yesterday in Kamata, I taught three (3) lessons, featuring a total of 8 young students between ages 6 and 11. The best thing is that in the nearby convenience store, I found my favorite candy bars still available! I saw them “everywhere” for a while, but then stopped seeing them.. It’s like they’re no longer being produced. boooo

  32. Still haven’t really nailed down our plans for the US. It’s kinda weird to have things to do during vacation; how can I plan shit if I have no idea what other fun things will be available?

  33. In general, I’m teaching 6 lesson hours per week, programming about 25 hours per week, most of which is done at home with the cats catting around the balcony door.

  34. Arriving in Kawasaki now; see ya in a sec while I chill out at the clock to wait for Lin.

  35. I did not end up buying a new computer a few months ago when I was preparing for this one to go to the shop for its end-of-warranty checkup. So, I’m still using my MacBook Pro, which is now about 3+ years old, I guess. I don’t know if I’ve made an entry for it in my Items: category on my wiki.

  36. I’m still using my iPhone 4S, with iOS 7.1 or so. The lastest models are iPhone 6S and iOS 9 or so. I’m sorta-kinda looking for a replacement phone; I will almost certainly go with Android.

    BUT, I don’t really want to give up this phone since I have an uncapped unlimited data plan for a fixed price. That plan is apparently no longer available, so I’m sure that when I switch phones, they’ll force me to pay for a different plan.

  37. I’ve recently purchased unlimited encrypted storage on SpiderOak, for $149 per year. It sounds great, but I don’t see a way to just toss data on their servers without it also residing on my device here. I guess if I have N computers, I can store all of their data separately; .. hmm maybe I’ll try to install a SpiderOak client on my not-really-used Ubuntu machine which is essentially just wasting away on my desk. (it’s the one I got as a possible (interim) replacement for this machine.)

  38. I need to pee, but Lin’s about to arrive in about 2 minutes haha

  39. Recently Hideo sent me an email with dates of a clinical trial in January, but we’ll be in the US. I guess I won’t be going down there again unless they have trials with an age limit above 45!

  40. I recently took apart a little ionizer thing that Lin said didn’t work anymore. I extracted its squirrel cage blower (did someone say generator??) and may play with it, but it’s pretty low priority now.

  41. I gave blood a bit more than three months ago, so I’ve just added an alarm to remind me to give blood again soon.
