primes

By Rob Nugen

prime examples

21 October 1998

After chatting with Jen last night, I shut down Windows and booted my linux partition. After a bit of playing, I found the following interesting numbers (among many many others) on my screen: 

666667
888887
987659
1000003
1001003
1234577
1357901
1444441
1777771
3333331
1777777777
214783647

"So?" you may ask.

Back you may press.

What are those numbers?

They are all prime numbers. Like 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, ... they can only be evenly divided by 1 and themselves.

Really?

Well, I don't know, but I trust the linux executable to know what it's doing.

214783647 was the largest number I could start it counting from. Any number larger than that to start and it would respond with "Ouch."

However, I piped the output through less and came up with 1777777777 as prime. Cool.

I let it run all night and the largest prime it found was 4294967291.
Nice.
