Great grief

By Rob Nugen

6:24pm JST Thursday 9 October 2003

Zoom from the shuttle toward the ticketing area.

"American Airlines?"

"Over there but the gate is closed. What is your destination? Dallas?"

"Hai."

headsetphone guy behind booth. bag X-rayed, baggage can't be checked, must be carried on, but still got checked bag tags just in case.

Zoom this way. I lift both janette's bags over my head to get past the turnstiles behind janette behind the guy. White shirt black vest red tie greets us. (This is it; quick hug kiss; "bye") Guy takes her black suitcase. Black vest zooms her through security express line. Wave bye.

Guy came back. "She's gonna make it on time."

"Domoarigatogozaimas."

janette still hasn't gone past the gates. Oh, there she goes. Wave bye through the glass.

Oh they're going past me. Quick pick before they descend the escalator.

"Bye."

Bang. Then it hits. Zoom to restroom, private stall. Cry cry cry.

- - - -

Okay. I'm okay.

Good thing is that we extended our time together to the max.

Bang. More crying.

Cute girls from Sweden. Buzz off. I give not a second glance.

Starbucks hot chocolate for comfort.

Speed-train to Yokohama = 4000+ yen. I have 4000 yen. 1000 yen train to Ueno, here I come.

I predict a pissed off day at work tomorrow.
