Amanda

By Rob Nugen

3:10pm JST Wednesday 12 November 2003

Amanda says, "say something funny? No, it's too difficult."

Earlier she cracked me up with her emphasis/accent on the word "heaving:"

"I went to Kyoto in April and it was absolutely heaving with people."

We've been buds on this Hakone day trip. She hates roller coasters, skydiving and anything scary. She's married, has no job and is a published journalist.

In short, we have nothing in common until we found that we both know a bit about the zodiac and have similar non-dramatic views of religion. (bad description, but meh (like our sense of religion))

We're about to arrive at the boat-stop in Hakone, but I don't think we'll make the departure in 3 minutes.

Nevermind; we will!

5:00 ish

Boat was cold. Big gondola up to the top of the mountain where it was even blusterier and colder.

Amanda says, "I like the fact that it was beautiful and you could see out to the Pacific and the sun was starting to set, although it was a bit misty."

At the souvenier shop, I took pictures of the trick box houses that I chose not to buy for Fred. We did however, get him another puzzle. I'll take a picture of it before I take it apart to give to him. I'll not let him have a pic of it; that cat is too good a putting together puzzles for any hints like what the finished product should look like.
