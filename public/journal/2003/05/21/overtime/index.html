overtime

By Rob Nugen

2:13pm JST Wednesday 21 May 2003

I'm working overtime today; I got called while I was in the internet cafe. I coulda turned it down; we never *have* to work overtime, but I felt like I didn't really have anything better to do except maybe study Japanese, so I said yes.

I'll be paid 1.5 times my normal rate. 5 classes * 1.5 = 7.5 classes so it's like working almost a full extra day. I got 1.25 times my rate yesterday (first day of overtime per week) so that's like working more than a day, though I only really worked 1/2 a day cause it was so unbusy.

What is the best use of my time? Why am I here? For this incredible experience

Back to the real question... wtf am I doing here? Do I make my priority working overtime? Meeting people and engaging the culture. I'm looking for some hard-and-fast rule on whether or not I "should" have said yes to the offer of overtime. As I write "offer" that suggests it was a good thing, so Yes is appropro (correct usage?).

It's silly that I look for hard and fast rules. Silly that I am saying I do something as if it's true.

Bryn once suggested that life is not as black and white as I seemed to view it during one of our conversations. I wonder how ol' Bryn is doing? I hope she's well and happy in London.

I am here for the experience. I'm here for the same reason that I believe we are here on earth. For the experience. Whatever I do is the right thing to do. I just suggest that I pay attention to what I'm doing - that I experience my life fully, even if I sometimes feel like I'm not wrestling from life every possible experience I *could* experience.

Just be; just live in the moment, moment to moment. Supa zen like.
