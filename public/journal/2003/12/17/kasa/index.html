It happened so quickly that I had no time to recognize what happened before I was on the ground in a heap. I don't even really remember the sequence... I guess, ah here it is: it happened so fast that there was none of this "it happened in slow motion" kinda stuff when my brain goes into hyper-processing mode and sees everything under the influence of lots of adrenaline (?). It was more like, [ride ride ridsplat]

. . . woah. Three second internal check: anything broken? No. any super sharp pain? No. Any dizziness? No. Any pain at all? yeah my wrist and my thigh.

I was a bit woozy (my head did not hit the ground) from having the wind knocked out of me (quite a strange term, and I wonder what the process really is and why it's so discombobulating) so I sat meditatively on the curb by my bicycle for a bit. Systematically took the umbrella out of my spokes, folded it and put it in my bag for show and tell. In a great ego-driven show of hugeness, I did not look around at who might be watching me (just hoped a hundred people were) and jumped back on my bike and sped toward the station.

In the station I called Miki to tell her I'd be late. My phone's display didn't survive the incident (I checked the time on the outer display, which was fine); I was able to call her from redial, not from (my) memory.

On the train after my dismount, I remembered the book _Mutant Messenger Down Under_ (I think) and healing-vibed my arm to heal quickly.
