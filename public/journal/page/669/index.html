your mothers pillow

By Rob Nugen

Your Mother’s Pillow

20 April 1998

This past weekend I attended a conference called "Your Mother's Pillow," designed and run by Char Tosi, the founder of Woman Within and Marlene Nappa, who I assume helped Char design the weekend experience.

I cannot possibly convey the magnitude of healing I received during the weekend.

My understanding is that men in this culture are often never properly weaned from their mother, and never learn to find mothering, nurturing energy within themselves. Men often seek this mothering in their adult relationships with other women. Maybe you have experienced this. I have.

For me, the great bigpowerfulwonderful experience created a space where I could take my adolescent self back from my mother. I learned that as a man, I can take care of my needs and the needs of the child part of me; I don't have to seek that source of nurturing elsewhere.

The whole experience was beyond description; I keep saying that... If you have any interest in healing childhood wounds, start with New Warrior Network if you are a man, and Woman Within if you are a woman.
Makes sense to me.

Good luck. It is hard work.

Relationships

Friday 17 April 1998

Wende is moving out soon; I am not ready for a "relationship", whatever that is.

Saturday at Marcel’s

Today I went to Marcel's parents' house to chill for the day. Mostly Marcel and I hung out the whole time, playing frisbee or shopping or inside with the family and Kayse and Adrien, plus Mimi's friend Caroline (?) who was in from Morocco.

  • 266 Mhz Pentium II
  • 6.4 Gig HD (didn't say SCSI or IDE)
  • 64 Meg memory
  • 24x CD Rom drive
  • 56k internal modem
  • 4 Megs memory on VGA card
  • sound card
  • $1500 !!

Oh, and ZIP drives (external, store 100megs on a disk) = $150 | Jaz (external 1 Gig) = $300 | Jaz (external 2 Gig) = $650

I looked because I'm in the market and I put it on my webpage for historical reference. I was really impressed with what is available!

Anyway, we didn't find what we needed, so we drove toward Best Buy, but chose to drive through the parking lot instead of using the street and bing we got to the end of the lot and there was a railroad track between us and Best Buy!

Cool! So we walked around on the tracks and spent several minutes analyzing how the electric current works around the switch. As total nerds, we had a great time, and you probably wouldn't understand.

After shopping, Marcel joked as we walked out of the brush back toward the car, "a cop sees us walking out of the bushes to the car, 'say, where'd you come from?' 'We parked here so we could walk across the tracks to go shopping! Really!'"

Back at his house we played frisbee outside for almost an hour, and I was amazed as the garbage truck went by picking up "garbage" for two reasons:

  1. What people will throw away (lots of aluminum cans and glass, bucket of paint, branches and leaves, 4x4 lumber about 5 feet long)
  2. The 4x4 was snapped almost like nothin' as the big scooper thing shoved the garbage toward its belly

Marcel's brother Andre joined us and we played a bit until it got too hot.

Then we dyed eggs once everyone got home from shopping and I recorded video of Marcel, Kayse and Adrien blowing bubbles and stuff. Then I recorded Mimi and Caroline and Mimi was all, "you can put that away" (don't record me!) so I told her I wasn't recording but just playing with the different picture effects.

I could click from "mosaic" to "sun filter" to "something" to "stretch" to "thin" to "B & W" to "photo negative" and that was cool. Oh, I was recording.

Had a great discussion with Marcel as I was leaving; we seem to do that- to start talkin' philosophical stuff when we are time constrained... I wonder why!

It was a great day with my friend.

Wow!

I was just listening to 107.5 the BUZZ, my favorite evening radio station (would be in the morning, but I hate the new DJ) and my man John Leach was giving away Third Eye Blind concert tickets to the next person who could show him a driver's license with the numbers, 1,0,7 & 5 in the number. Wow! Wow!

So they'll be goin' to the concert together and have a good time and a couple beers..."

I was pumped!

But wait, before I got home (yes my car was fine on the sidewalk), I remembered "d'oh! I'm s'posed to meet Kathy at 8pm!"

Aw man... So I called her up and left a cancellation type message. I hated to do that, but I hope she'll understand when I tell the whole story.

So now I'm gonna get ready and go!

Ministry With Youth

Friday 3 April 1998

Tonight began Renaissance Module: Ministry With Youth, a weekend workshop designed for Unitarian Universalist religious educators to learn how to support youth and youth advisors in creating a safe space to empower youth.

The most amazing thing I learned tonight was after we ended for the night. Christina Branum-Martin, one of my best friends from my YRUU days actually created what we now know as the Leadership Development Conference or LDC!! Just several years ago, as a youth, she and Duane Dowell designed them! I feel so honored that Christina is my friend and I am actually kinda jealous/awe-inspired by her work!

I want to create something like that.

Oh... the SWD YRUU website. Yeah, but an annual conference that is now used across the continent!

Sigh...

I am inspired. I really want to just quit my job and work doing what I love.

By Rob Nugen

Chuck E. Cheese

Wednesday 1 April 1998

At Chuck E. Cheese's Magical Satanical Pizza Place, the goal for the night was to acquire enough tickets to trade in for a lava lamp, lofting large purple wax bubbles bathed in soft light behind the counter.

We included "Satanical" in the name because their phone number includes "666" but the recording we hear when we dial the number says something like, "Thank you for calling Chuck E. Cheese's Magical Pizza Place..."
Okay, well, it's funny to us, so laugh!

Anyway, after eating pizza and mixing soft drinks to taste like Nerds, we hit the gameroom. I had like $9 in tokens and spent them all on one machine that lets me use a little gun shaped remote-flipper to toss one coin at a time nearly randomly into a glass box with dinosaur heads and crevices and a spinning Wheel of Fortune looking thing at the bottom. The crevices were labeled with different ticket amounts between 1 and 25 tickets. The dinsaur heads were worth either 100 or 200 tickets, and the Wheel of Fortune had about 20 slots awarding between 1 and 25 tickets.

In just several minutes, I converted my 36 tokens into 273 tickets.

Sounds great until doing the math. Each ticket is worth 1 penny at the counter, while the tokens were worth 36 tokens * 25 cents per token = 900 pennies.

But the story is a lot more interesting than, "I went to Chuck's and bought a 1 dollar flashlight for 9 bucks."

Gambling fever took kT most viciously at what we call the "Feed Me Tokens" game. It's the one where you toss tokens or quarters in there and little shovers shove the enormous stack to within one tenth of one millimeter of falling off the edge, but nothing falls.

About 8 tokens went into the machine and a few tokens fell and about 10 tickets came out. Then four quarters went in and one token fell and 10 more tickets were ours. Dennis set off the TILT alarm probably 10 times trying to help.

Out of tokens, we went and weighed our loot of tickets.

Including the previous 2 nights kT and Dennis had gone, including around 300 tickets I donated, they had 1270 tickets.

$12.70 for maybe 80 or 100 bucks in tokens? Good for Chuck!

So we (me, Laura, kT, Dennis) dove into the ball crawl and stormed through the tunnels and tossed balls at one another at buried ourselves and ambushed the others and generally had a great time.

kT found 4 tokens in the bottom of the ball crawl!

We converted them into like 10 tickets, gazing longingly at the precarious stacks in Feed Me Tokens.
