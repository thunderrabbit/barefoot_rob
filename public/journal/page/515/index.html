Monsters Inc

By Rob Nugen

12:01am CST Monday 5 November 2001

Monsters, Inc was *brilliant*!!!

You absolutely must see this movie because (in no particular order):

  • It was funny
  • It was fully computer animated
  • The writing was brilliant
  • All of the above reasons again

I totally loved this film. I thought all the good stories were written, and this one comes along and produces a whole new setting, ready for a million new stories to come out of it.

The chase scene near the end of the movie was great roller coastery fun. Go see it!

Oh! And, speaking of new stories, Star Wars Episode II, Attack of the Clones trailer appeared before Monsters, Inc. The entire soundtrack of the trailer was Darth Vader's breath while we watched varied and random scenes. Then we hear vwack vwack of two light sabers and that was it. More movie brilliance.

Bridges

By Rob Nugen

8:54am Friday 2 November 2001

I was surprised to see a cop car on both sides of the Hazard Street bridge as I drove to work. I stopped and asked "What are you doing?"

"guarding the bridge."

"I was afraid you were going to say that."

If a vehicle stops on the bridge, they are to block traffic from entering the bridge (and I guess confront the guy or whatever). If a vehicle stops under the bridge, same thing. Not that he can see what's going on under the bridge, but the other cops on the other bridges can. Wow.

This is apparently the mayor/police chief/other public officials covering their ass after 4 bridges in San Francisco have been targeted. As of now, they are intact and apparently well guarded.

War sucks.

Apartment

By Rob Nugen

First day in apartment

9:32pm CST Tuesday 30 October 2001

Today just after we lost internet connectivity at work, I cruised over to the apartment place and signed the lease. It took a lot longer than I expected, but it's all good. By the time I got back to work, we still didn't have internet access.

It felt strange to be poring over all the legalese in the lease. Strange to be reading a lease at all. I vaguely remembered the same experience from 1989 when Tammy and I moved into our apartment in the Greenspoint area. I think I like this complex a lot better - it seems homelier and friendlier.

This evening after ASL, I dropped off a box of books (my journals, actually), and my plumeria, which now has about 20 leaves. I met Kay, Donna, Karen (I think those were their names.) Kay was moving out because the place was getting too rowdy - just the opposite of what I heard from the manager and stuff. We'll see how it goes.

My phone number will stay the same. I'm thankful for that. :-)
