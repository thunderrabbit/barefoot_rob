Work

By Rob Nugen

12:01am JST Friday 19 December 2003

At work I was minding my own business in the teachers' room on a free lesson when one of the staff came in, "Robu, student is waiting." and I had a momentary freakout that maybe I had read the schedule wrongly, but I hadn't; there was no name on the timeslot. J-lo was there to back me up (he's an AT (I think) or something) so that was cool; he handled the staff, and I went to the class and had a chat with the student (who hadn't brought his book and didn't want to use my book, but did want to level up) and I was like, "dawg, you gotta pick up the pace if you want to get to level 4" and I didn't tell him that he shouldn't even be in level 5, but more like he's a 6 with a dangerous vocabulary and tries to overcomplicate everything, which I subsequently learned he was intentionally trying to overcomplicate so that his structures would be worthy of level 5.

I like to think that my advice will be helpful for him. Keep it simple and speed it up.

I explained that if the sentence is long and beautiful but takes an hour to say, we teachers are like, "wtf was the first part of that sentence; I can't remember it anymore..." and I think he understood.

I made him tell me stories and then I told the same story in 30 to 45 seconds that took him 4 or 5 minutes and I made him tell me the same story again in under a minute. That helped his fluency some. In addition, I shortened his circumlocuitous manner of speaking.

- - - -

Saw かおり, KG みき (there to sign up for more lessons), a different woman than かおり's みき who seemed to be hanging out with かおり though I don't remember ever having met her before, and ゆたか, who apparently was in Morag's Club 7 voice on shopping.

- - - -

I learned the names of なおひろ, ともなお, and りゅうじ, three level 6 men in my last class. I am making a concerted effort to remember male students' names as well.

- - - -

Had a chat with our level 2 student のりこ, whose name I can now remember. I didn't remember until she mentioned that she's only been out of Japan once (to Waikiki and San Francisco) but her English is realllly good grammatically and structurally, but doesn't have the speed or intonation of a native.
