bryn

By Rob Nugen

9pm ish

Bryn and I were sitting next to one another looking at her pictures of London and Paris when the lights went out as we were beckoned to the mass wedding.

Not an *actual* wedding, but an invitation for each person to honor others in friendship - these incredible bonds that YRUU makes possible.

This activity started in Plano a few years ago, and they had an anniversary each year afterward. Since YRUU has such a high turnover rate, most youth here now weren't at the original event, making anniversaries a bit silly, so this year, another mass wedding.

I really wanted to marry Bryn, but didn't know if we would be interrupted by other youth who wanted to marry me, or, and this was my greater expectation, that many people would want to marry Bryn.

Then we were in the circle and Bryn asked if I would marry her. "It would be my greatest pleasure," I smiled.

Doug Strong introduced the ceremony, then invited individuals who were marrying themselves to place left arm on right shoulder and right arm on left shoulder.

Pairs of people were invited to put one hand on each others shoulder, and groups of people to form a group hug. Then people who intended to marry everyone come to the middle into the light.

Bryn and I faced each other with hands on shoulders and were approached by Mike and Lisa to be a foursome. Bryn looked to me, asking with her eyes. I was all, "ummmememrrr," keeping my hands on Bryn's shoulders. It was so hard to turn them down, and I certainly didn't do it cleanly, but we remained a couple and Mike and Lisa joined the marry-everyone group when that option was offered. (*)

Doug invited us to share with one another our passions.

"Travel, Dance, Painting, YRUU, smiles," me.

"Travel, family, love...," she.

We all had been given inexpensive little gold rings. When instructed, Bryn put hers on my left pinky finger. I chose her right ring finger, though both her ring fingers sported rings already. Some more words from Doug and then, "I now pronounce you married!"

And we hugged so big and I just started to cry.

"Are you laughing?"

"No."

Cried cried so much passion for Bryn and life and love and for five incredible years of knowing, loving, supporting, encouraging, teaching each other..

Bryn began to cry as well. Gentle sobs and tears. She was all, "Thank you for the last five years...." and then, "you have been the only person who has been here for me the entire time." more precious tears from each of us.

It has been my utmost pleasure to be here for you Bryn. Then I was all, "in the past year it has become perfectly clear to me that I have been in YRUU for you."

Huge tears from Bryn. Huge huge tears and sobs. So thankful to be perfectly here for Bryn.

I chose not to say anything about having been at Emerson YRUU specifically for Jon. This is Bryn's moment. My moment with Bryn. I love you love you so much.

We had held each other tightly for already five minutes at this point, and for easily ten more as we just cried. My tears completed and I just held my dear Bryn to capture this moment clearly in my heart and mind for all eternity.

Re-structured our hug so I was on her level. More huge hugs.

People came up to me and Bryn are you okay? "Yes" and (thumbs up) and Simon wanted to hug Bryn cause she was crying.. and he innocently asked what was wrong.

I answered. "This has been like me and Bryn's Senior circle We are leaving YRUU soon, and have enjoyed five incredible years together. Bryn is going to school in August, and next year I'm going to Japan."

Simon was all, "no; you can't leave!"

"You'll go too," I hugged him bigly. "Anywhere you want, you can go."

Later, Ian was all, "thank you for being so awesome at Fort Worth Rally."

I thanked him for the blessing and the opportunity to be there for him.

March 2015

(*) I still sometimes think about turning them down. I’m sorry I was so selfish and wish I could go back to that moment and include them as well.
