Dream featuring Andrew Basiago

By Rob Nugen

##06:31 Wednesday 07 January 2015 JST##

Andrew Basiago was looking for an assistant and I applied for the job. He knew me already, so I just wrote Rob Nugen on the list because I figured he already had my contact information. For a while I thought I should have written it just to be sure, but it was okay. There were several other people in the line, and I glanced over and saw my name on the list he had written so I guesed it was fine. The crowds thinned out and Andrew motioned to me that we could go up to have our meeting in his office. His house was two stories in a neighborhood within walking distance of my house. I had my cat Jennie with me but didn't think I should take her into his house, so I planned to just leave her outside, which my wife wouldn't appreciate, so I said, "hey I better take Jennie home" and Andrew agreed, and encouraged me, as always, to do the right thing. I caught Jennie as she was running down the wooden steps from his house and walked her to my house. Inside, I got sidetracked, on something tangentially related, like writing in my diary that I had met Andrew, and during that moment I forgot he was actually waiting for me. I thought _oh no I was supposed to get right back!_ I thought Andrew would be upset, so I started to rush back. As I exited my house, there a moving van was arriving outside, and I remembered that someone was moving in to replace a housemate (sister?) who was moving out. I realized I should wait to tell them where to move the stuff, but then saw lots of people were there as well, and I realized someone else could show them. On the way to Andrews's house again, I was climbing up a steep (45 degree) slope made of 2x4 boards, and a young girl started playfully chasing me up. I didn't want to engage her so I tried to hurry, but realized that would be rude, so I let her catch me. She confided to me a note she had written about food, which she dared not show her parents. It started with "shit: " and went on about food that she didn't like to eat, and had several lines drawn between the food categories broccoli, epeas, asparagus, etc. that she didn't like. I asked her if I could have it and she said it was for me. I refolded it (in half widthwise and then in half twice the other direction). I walked down the white sandstone slab steps down the hill toward Andrew's front door and found my computer which I had left on top of the steps. I started to cry a bit because I had left something so important to me outside and forgotten about it. I picked it up with thanks that it was still there and Andy opened the door by the time I got to his porch. I apologized for thinking I'd be right back and explained that the girl had given me a cute note. He unfolded it on his glass desk and the paper spread out into a map of the city with her writing still on it. He asked, "so where are we focusing?" and then he noticed her writing again in the center of the map. An overhead camera displayed the map on his computer screen, using software processing to flatten it out as he unfolded it. I was like, "wow! It would take me hours to scan that whole thing" Andrew agreed and said that's why he bought the overhead scanning system. He asked me what title I wanted for my role as his assistant. I didn't know this was an option, so I just repeated what I had seen somewhere else, "PG?" and he said, "are you sure you want Programmer?" for that's what PG meant. I was like, "ohhh!" because I hadn't known what the PG meant and thought about it for a while. I thought of TT, Time Traveler, but figured that would be too obvious. I came up with "TE" for Time Enhancer, meaning that I wanted to help everyone improve the quality of their experience of time, to be more fully present in their moments of experience.

I began to wake up and reviewed the dream in my mind so I could recall it for my journal. Lin’s alarm went off, bringing me more fully awake so I jumped up to write the dream before I lost it.
