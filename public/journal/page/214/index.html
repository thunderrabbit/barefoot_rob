fixed screen door

By Rob Nugen

13:57 Monday 30 March 2015 JST

When we got our cats last year, I replaced the thin, easily-ripped screen on the sliding door with a somewhat sturdier stainless steel screen.

After the cats ascended the screen a few times, leaving claw-sized sags, they realized the could just ram through the bottom corner, so they did.

I deliberated for a long time how to replace it with something even sturdier, and have not yet found a solution. Even so, Lin was like “yeah, if you could fix the screen, that’d be great.”

I was able to reuse the existing screen just be turning it upside down. And actually it seems like it will work pretty well.

Here’s what I wrote to Apple when the dialog said “oh you killed Mail app; do you want to report it?”

Finder won’t start.

I don’t know if that’s affecting Mail, but I had to kill finder
because its one (1) single window couldn’t display ten (10) ten
little (less than 1MB each) images.  It just hangs with a blank
white page and spinning icon.  I can see the images with terminal;
I can see them with emacs.  I can see them with Cyberduck.  But
not Finder.

Why can’t I see them with Finder? 

I’ll stop here, but I swear on my million times I’ve yelled at my
computer I will never buy Apple gear again.

Funnily enough, I discovered I couldn’t use Finder while I was trying to upload a new icon to itunesconnect for our latest app.

Right now the top command is using the most CPU, at 10%.

Chrome is now locked up while trying to quick-look an image that I think is the correct one I need to upload.

10:29am
