modulations

By Rob Nugen

modulations

Monday 14 December 1998


(day 10491)

Last night Manda and I saw the movie _Modulations_. From their website: "Modulations traces the evolution of electronic music as one of the most profound artistic developments of the 20th century."

Modulations was certainly super rad; it made me really really want to go check out a rave *NOW* but I had no idea where one might be on Sunday night. It was just about the right mix of sound/light vs technical and historical documentary, but I would have liked more (and louder) on the soundtrack part. They didn't go too much into the minds of the rave crowds, but described pretty thoroughly the music. Like where "house music" started and why it's called that. And how the British scene changed and resulted in the creation of jungle music.

(I'm not giving away the answers (unless you ask) so I won't ruin the movie for you)

They had lots of interviews of different djs and magazine writers. I only knew a 2 of the names: dj spooky and invisible skratch picklz

If the rave scene or electronic music intrigues you, go check out the film.

By Rob Nugen

Little Rock Rally

4-6 December 1998

Leaving Houston at 9:30am, I drove kT Hammons and Nicole Mauldin up to Tulsa to pick up Frank Roesler and Eryn Pope. I had forgotten to get a map to the Tulsa church, but we stopped by a McDonald's and used their phone book and figured it out.

Said HI to Ashley Robinson, Stacey Craig, and a couple others after I took a quick nap at the Tulsa church. Then we were on our way after a quick stop at Wendy's for food.

Frank cracked me up and I almost snarfed with his hilarious beat box sounds. Had to be there.

Got to Little Rock at about midnight. Signed in and hugged and said HI to everyone and opening circle Matt and Mari Mock and Gene Hudnall and Nick Gruenewald made all the announcements and stuff. A game of Mafia followed, and it was super cool because we who were Mafia, happened to all be right next to each other in the circle! (Except for 1 person who we killed first)

It was me, Eryn Pope, Bryan Beck, Danny K, and uh oh.. I'm in trouble. I don't remember who else was mafia. Mafia won, since Bryan volunteered himself to die and threw off the whole group.

Then I did sleep sleep sleep almost through breakfast and then watched Pulp Fiction and then hung out on the porch with Bryan and Eryn near JoEllen's spirituality workshop. Aaron Ivener showed up with one of his friends a chatted for a bit.

Yoga workshop then massage workshop, where I volunteered to be massaged by the woman doing the workshop. Yay!

Eryn and I played with Jenga blocks, seeing how high we could stack them. Then a dude whose name I forgot brought a bunch of drums and stuff and taught us some beats and a dance and all kinds of fun.

Then Nick G stacked up the Jenga blocks in the highest stack we had been able to do and we took some pictures of it. I went to bed, but apparently he placed one of those black music stands on top of it! There are rumors that he got a picture of it, and other rumors that it fell off and hit him on the head first.

Slept through talent show (sorry, guys) and no Silent Football (you could have played without me!) and woke up at 4am for a brief walk around the church then back to bed for a few hours.

Slept through cleanup and then we snagged breakfast and closing circle sang Yogi Bear and good friends and stuff.

Bright yellow covers on the mugbooks, but I've lost mine before I could put it in the Big Gray Rally Book. Pooh. Wonderful fun rally!

scanned an image

By Rob Nugen

I scanned an image!

19 September 1998

Wow! I finally was able to get Windows 95 going enough that I could scan an image! The weird thing however, is that I can't get Windows to properly support my monitor. It will only go to 640X480 pixels in 16 colors.

10 years ago that woulda been great!

But anyway, I scanned this image, uploaded it to my site, exited Windows, went to bed early (10pm), woke up (it's 5:34am), booted Linux, used GIMP (Gnu Image Manipulation Program) to adjust the color of the image, and re-uploaded it.

And bing here's the result! The scanner seemed to have some problem with streaking or something, but hey -- *I* can tell it's me!

This picture was taken in August 1993 by Jeanette, my second cousin from Phoenix who attends school in Tucson. We were at the Phoenix airport and I felt rushed to get onto the plane on time and I was all, "oh Jeanette hurry!" but she made me pose by the fountain.

I made it to the plane on time. I love the picture. :-) Hugs to Jeanette for encouraging me to take a moment.

(now I gotta find her email address!)

So yes, I may soon upload some more images that I've been promising.. I certainly look forward to making the process a bit shorter. Maybe I can get Thomas to figure out why my monitor drivers won't work.
