GirlScoutCookieTimeAgain

By Rob Nugen

11:46pm CST Wednesday 16 January 2002

I may suddenly have some insight as to why I can get more work done when no one is here to bother me.

Earlier this morning I heard the doorbell ring (meaning someone came in our suite), that someone talked to Deborah about Girl Scout Cookies.

Five minutes ago, Karla walked into our office (where Minh, Dave and I work) and gave me the GSC order form, "here, you can order Girl Scout Cookies; pass it around when you are done."

Two minutes ago, after ordering 1 box of Thin Mints, I gave the form to Minh without a word (but took some of the crackers he had been snacking on). Minh said, "that's a good trade." (a few crackers for boxes of cookies (granted he has to pay for the cookies, but whatever.))

Minh called his girlfriend on the phone, "Baby, do you want some Girl Scout Cookies? Thin Mints? How many boxes? I'm ordering some right now. Okay, bye."

Then Minh gave the list to Dave, who said, "oh, is it Girl Scout Cookie time again?"

HOW DID HE NOT NOTICE ANY OF THE PREVIOUS INTERACTIONS???????

Why do I notice (and sometimes get distracted by) everything around me?

At one level I'm thinking, "holy shit, man, pay attention!" and on another level I'm envious that he can apparently focus, and on another level I'm wondering do I really notice a lot, or do I just think I notice a lot?. Hmmmmmm.

I don't know, but I'm now going to nin din work mode.
