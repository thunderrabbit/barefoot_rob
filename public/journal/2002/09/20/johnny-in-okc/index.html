Johnny in OKC

By Rob Nugen

9:07am CDT Friday 20 September 2002

Good morning from Sean Murray's apartment in OKC. Thanks to Tiffany for making phone calls last night to get me here even though Sean is not. I got to meet his roommate Johnny from Germany, who is going to Nepal next year to teach English!

Didn't talk to him super much last night, but got here with no problem. Ironic is that while I was at Kinko's, I was like 1 mile from this apartment. Awesome.

1:17pm

Cooked breakfast for Johnny the birthday boy today, who's now 20 years old. Just went with him to Sean's grandparents' house and chatted with them a bit before going to The Dive Shop, where Johnny picked up gear to do his final open water dive to be certified this Sunday morning at 8am at Lake Murray. Now we're going to go ride bikes.

I'm loving this!

2:57pm

Tired; rode around the nearby lake a bit, and I noticed my energy like whoah nearly sapped after a bit of an exertion riding up a bit of a hill and climbing over a wall. Maybe I shoulda eaten more than two eggs and an apple.

Flew down a grassy hill from a highway overpass and trudged through slightly tall grass up a slight hill. I will keep up bike riding so this slight exertion won't sap my energy so easily.

Johnny is talking to his peeps in German, very interesting to hear him say "Hello?" to answer the phone and then "Alloh?" when he heard German on the other side.

7:05pm

Just ate dinner at a thai place with Johnny and his girlfriend Sabra. We were cutting up at the end and he offered me some of his glass noodle enriched meal. Then Sabra asked if she could have some as she reached for his plate. He was all, "I didn't say you could have some," and she pulled back. Then I reached over and offered her some of his meal.

"So can I have some or not?" asked Sabra.

"Of course; he offered it to you and he's bigger than me, so what am I going to do?" joked Johnny.

Also, on the menu were two interesting names of dishes: "Noodles a la Nick" and "Row Boat Beef and Noodles." Amazing how YRUUers got their names on foods in a thai restaurant.
