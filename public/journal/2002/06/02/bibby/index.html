Bibby

By Rob Nugen

10:44 pm Saturday 1 June 2002

Back at the Bibby's after the CYA meeting. Paul, Sean, Tera, Jo, Hillary, Jack. And me.

Jo is on the phone though, so we are waiting until she's done to watch pulp fiction.

1:43 am 2-6-02

Just finished watching Pulp Fiction. Joey says, "awesome," and "no you shut up."

Sean says, "uhh, I think uh, this summer is uh, the summer of The Bibbster."

fuckin up the way a nigga talks.

2:20 am

Tired now. Going to bed.

12:26am Wednesday 5 June 2002

It's been a few days since the weekend adventure, but I'm just now getting it written. I brought my bike so that Sean and I could ride around. We joked back and forth several times, "guess what I got?" "What?" "A mountain bike!!" "Really? Me too!!" "We should go ride sometime!"

We rode out by White Lake (?) about a mile+ from his house. I rode on some crazy not so flat pile of rocks. Mostly flat, but with big wheel sized gaps between some of them. Very hard to ride. I also rode dangerously on a little wall with a chain link fence on one side and a 5 foot drop on the other. I'm glad Sean was smart enough not to follow. :-)

I loved meeting Sean and Joey's mom. She's quite cool and seems to have some similar spiritual beliefs/practices as I. I look forward to visiting with her again.

Fun getting lost with Chezly and Sean on the way to drop Chez off at her house. I got to see her green room and we both got a copy of her defunct band's CD single.

I didn't really get to talk with Hillary, Paul, Tera or Jack, but they were definitely there. Most def.

Super thanks to all the youth for a great time!

Oh, I almost forgot... The next day with youth sunday at First Jefferson in Fort Worth. I got to participate in the bridging ceremony, which people seemed to greatly enjoy. Talked with Katie Lu, Laura D, Nathan Sexton, Asha, Lauren Olson, et al.

Josephine told a great knock knock joke:

Knock knock?
Who's there?
Brittany Spears.
Brittany Spears who?
Knock knock?
Who's there?
Brittany Spears.
Brittany Spears who?
Oops, I did it again!

On the way back to Bibbster's, I got to see all of Rick's photos of his amazing tile mosaics and such. Quite impressive work. I'd like to see some in person sometime.
