cluster kickoff

By Rob Nugen

10 May 1998

Today was Flower Communion at my church. The service stems from way back in Czechloslavakia when Norbert Capek (The 'C' character is actually a C with a v on it.) a Unitarian minister, wanted to have a communion but not using bread and wine.
That's the short version of the story.

But that's not why I'm writing.

Today after church, Mary McCuen and I, en route to Einstein Bros. Bagels, talked about activities for the summer. This Wednesday for our LRY dinner, we're going to invite all the youth from the seven local UU churches to meet so we can plan some dates for meetings during the summer!

I'm super excited about actually doing to cluster level stuff, getting the group together doing stuff together... I'm excited about the possibilities.

So I called Christina Branum-Martin and she said she was interested in having youth worships during the summer. We now plan to have a worship in the evening of each first Sunday of the month. Yay!

I'm doing the smiley bouncy dance just thinking about it!

1998 Oklahoma City Rally (in) review by Bryn "Bean" Cain

First thing I remember was Eryn Pope, Austin Roth, Nora and I were all sitting outside in the courtyard around 8-8:30 just layin on each others tummies talkin, Nora is from Germany (as you all know) so we were askinh er all sorts of silly questions about Germany, and Austin was talkin about tummies, and it was just jolly fun.

Finally he made his wy down the tree, and just sat with us till more people arrived. The next people to arrive were the Mckeons and well, half of Fort Worth. They went in and took their pictures, and we all sat down to Susanne's wonderful guitar playing. We sang Cat Stevens, and the Beatles mostly, with a little extra stuff thrown in on the sides. Noah was playing my drum, and Misty (the dog from Stillwater) was just runnin around being cute, I love to sing, and Susanne is great, so that exceptionally nice.

After a while, and all at one time it seems, a whole big group of faces got here from San An and Fort Worth. One of them my SWUUSI '96 friend Nathan Sexton from Fort Worth. Not sure what happended next, but eventually, Nathan, Clark, Andy Artus, Austin Artus, Gavin, and I all started playing football, with a tiny little thing that was eventually popped *but not by us* Gavin and Clark tryed multiple times to catch the ball while skateboarding..... it didn't work too well, but man did they look hilarious.

After a while of that, a frisbee appeared and we ran to make its aquaintance. Nathan and I played a while, until Misty insisted on playing. Nathan tryed throwing the frisbee to Misty, but after she caught it she wouldn't give it back to him. They wrestled around with the frisbee for a while, until eventually Nathan threw the frisbee and we ran inside to hide.

A few of us then went into the "loungin" room to watch anime. I don't really like anime, but someone was giving me a back massage so I was happy. I was giggling like crazy, because I'm so ticklish, it was a lot better than I thought it would be, but oh my goodness, it was crazy! The about 15 minutes later FRANK PHUUFed me too!!!!! Misty came up and snuggled my leg which made me feel all happy because she hadn't been nice to me all weekend! But the talent show was exceptional.

They are all seriously psychotic. They went back and forth accusing each other of this and that forever, and even forgot to charge me with using the most hanous [bad spelling] pronoun "he". (YEAH) At this point, I had 4 and a half penace points, and everyone else was close behind. But all of a sudden, some source from within the universe started making the gravitational pull around my head jerk up and down, taking my head with it. My head started bobbin', and before I knew it, I had a penace point (thanks Kris) and I had lost the game!!!!! I was tired though, so I didn't mind much. My punishment was to sing "one little two little three little indians", which I didn't know the ending to, at closing circle (oops, I forgot.)

After clearing out, I was tired, so I went in the loungin room, and went to sleep. Someone woke me up about 30 minutes later, as it was 7:00, and time to wake up and clean. I cleaned fast, and went to lay down in the loungin room with Katie and Keith. We just chilled for about an hour talking and snoozin'.

Then it was time for closing circle. I was getting sad at the thought of departure of all my new-found friends, but as always, I had to say goodbye. ministry with youth

By Rob Nugen

Ministry With Youth

Friday 3 April 1998

Tonight began Renaissance Module: Ministry With Youth, a weekend workshop designed for Unitarian Universalist religious educators to learn how to support youth and youth advisors in creating a safe space to empower youth.

Tera Little and Lisa Rubens are the facilitators for the weekend, and that's cool because Tera is an advisor/DRE from my district and she's really great! We went as advisors to many rallies last year.

This night's work has really encouraged me to just go for it and create my life around youth work. Just give me room and board and access to the Web and I'll do it!

The most amazing thing I learned tonight was after we ended for the night. Christina Branum-Martin, one of my best friends from my YRUU days actually created what we now know as the Leadership Development Conference or LDC!! Just several years ago, as a youth, she and Duane Dowell designed them! I feel so honored that Christina is my friend and I am actually kinda jealous/awe-inspired by her work!

I want to create something like that.

Oh... the SWD YRUU website. Yeah, but an annual conference that is now used across the continent!

Sigh...

I am inspired. I really want to just quit my job and work doing what I love.

lry chuck e cheese

By Rob Nugen

Chuck E. Cheese

Wednesday 1 April 1998

At Chuck E. Cheese's Magical Satanical Pizza Place, the goal for the night was to acquire enough tickets to trade in for a lava lamp, lofting large purple wax bubbles bathed in soft light behind the counter.

We included "Satanical" in the name because their phone number includes "666" but the recording we hear when we dial the number says something like, "Thank you for calling Chuck E. Cheese's Magical Pizza Place..."
Okay, well, it's funny to us, so laugh!

Anyway, after eating pizza and mixing soft drinks to taste like Nerds, we hit the gameroom. I had like $9 in tokens and spent them all on one machine that lets me use a little gun shaped remote-flipper to toss one coin at a time nearly randomly into a glass box with dinosaur heads and crevices and a spinning Wheel of Fortune looking thing at the bottom. The crevices were labeled with different ticket amounts between 1 and 25 tickets. The dinsaur heads were worth either 100 or 200 tickets, and the Wheel of Fortune had about 20 slots awarding between 1 and 25 tickets.

In just several minutes, I converted my 36 tokens into 273 tickets.

Sounds great until doing the math. Each ticket is worth 1 penny at the counter, while the tokens were worth 36 tokens * 25 cents per token = 900 pennies.

But the story is a lot more interesting than, "I went to Chuck's and bought a 1 dollar flashlight for 9 bucks."

Gambling fever took kT most viciously at what we call the "Feed Me Tokens" game. It's the one where you toss tokens or quarters in there and little shovers shove the enormous stack to within one tenth of one millimeter of falling off the edge, but nothing falls.

About 8 tokens went into the machine and a few tokens fell and about 10 tickets came out. Then four quarters went in and one token fell and 10 more tickets were ours. Dennis set off the TILT alarm probably 10 times trying to help.

Out of tokens, we went and weighed our loot of tickets.

Including the previous 2 nights kT and Dennis had gone, including around 300 tickets I donated, they had 1270 tickets.

$12.70 for maybe 80 or 100 bucks in tokens? Good for Chuck!

So we (me, Laura, kT, Dennis) dove into the ball crawl and stormed through the tunnels and tossed balls at one another at buried ourselves and ambushed the others and generally had a great time.

kT found 4 tokens in the bottom of the ball crawl!

We converted them into like 10 tickets, gazing longingly at the precarious stacks in Feed Me Tokens.

rally dallas 1998

By Rob Nugen

Dallas Rally 1998

6-8 February 1998

Dallas Rally was last weekend!

Once we got a bit more than halfway to Dallas, kT and I thoroughly confounded Laura and Adam with a nice long game of Ceiling Wall Floor.

I was pleased that I didn't have to consult the map to get to the church. I've been to Dallas Rally a few times!

We got there with bouncy happy anticipation, signed in, dumped our stuff, got mugbook pictures taken (I wore my rainbow striped stocking cap over my face for the picture), and I walked around saying HI to everyone.

I missed the first part of opening circle; I don't remember why.

After opening circle, the activity level was kinda chill. By 3am many people were all crashed out sleeping around the sanctuary. I wandered from group to group until like 4am and slept till 7.

The funniest thing about the Games Workshop Saturday AM was that Bryan Beck didn't know it was an actual workshop. He had joined us in order to not attend a workshop! Once he found out, he almost left, but we convinced him to stay. We played Ceiling Wall Floor, Telephone, and the 7 game.

Lunch was yummy sandwiches, and I got to be nearly first in line so people on SWUUSI staff could go to the SWUUSI staff meeting. I ended up not going to the meeting, but the sandwich was great!

I attended Will B's acting workshop in afternoon. He taught us part of how to really "get into character." We chose individual monologues and read them aloud. Then we decided what was the character's favorite color, day of the week, type of music, car, house, etcetera, and reread them. Amazing how much more real that made the reading!

Soon after that, several of us played Ultimate in the YMCA field. I had great fun, and I hope everyone else did too.

After almost half an hour of Ultimate, the YAC members had to leave to attend the YAC meeting, which was actually quite productive, despite Bryan B's crazy sleep-deprived antics and the general non-focused short attention spans we all seemed to have.

I bounced around the various groups in Channing Hall and the sanctuary, making sure all my youth were still happy and healthy. They were, and the energy level of the rally was kinda slowing down, so I took a nap for about an hour until time for worship.

I woke up and soon my brother arrived! I knew he was planning to come, but it was super cool to see him. We hadn't been to a rally together since Dallas Rally 1990.. I introduced Fred (my bro) around to lots of people, and surprise surprise, he worked on remembering everyone's names. After a while, I quit introducing him as my brother, because of what we planned for the talent show.

The funniest thing: Fred and I were looking for someone to beatbox as part of our talent show. Curtis M would drop some beats as an example to whoever we asked if they could beatbox. (I'm laffin' as I type this, but I can tell it's not as funny in text.)
We'd be all, "Hey, Ryan, can you beatbox?"
And Ryan (or whoever we asked) would be all, "like what?"
"Curtis, show 'em." And Curtis would bust out some trippin' beat for about 2 seconds and we'd go, "do that."
They'd be "what??? I can't do that!"
We couldn't do it either; don't worry.

Worship was another Nathan Ryan Original, and quite pleasing to my spirit. Ya had to be there!

After worship we meandered and then congregated in Channing Hall for the talent show.

After several great acts, Fred and I did ours: Things to Prove We are Brothers. Jeremy had a list of items that he could read and Fred and I would each have the same response. They were funny (or not) because the reactions were so out of context and some were just bizarre. The final one Jeremy read was "Alright" to which Fred and I rapped our beloved alphabet game twice, with Curtis et. al. doing beatbox as backup.

As we finished, Tiffany S and Suzanne K and Maybe Maia C were all, "wait! We want to see the feet!" They wanted Fred to take off his shoes to see if he had hobbit feet like me. After a bit of balking, Fred took off his shoes and bang sure enough he has hair on his feet too!

Adam L then was all, "wait! There's one more thing you need to compare!" So, after Kristian S and Aaron I's performance of Madonna's "Like A Prayer." Fred and I performed our belt skit. Again, you had to be there.

Talented folks included Kristian S and Aaron I playing guitars, Eric M playing guitar as Sean M belched the melody, Steve F playing "Rocky Raccoon," Nancy D reading some of her poetry, Nick G lip synching Coolio's "Gangsta's Paradise," Suzanne K and Tiffany S singing a couple songs, Lauren I reading a poem, Jeremy L-N singing "I will survive," Amy playing flute, Devon singing, and Robert playing piano.

At 3:AM the talent show kinda ended, and I must say that was the truly most talented talent show I've seen at a Rally for a long time. Thanks to all who were involved!

At about 3:30 we started a game of Silent Football. I was chosen as dictator, and dangit, I can't remember everyone who played. Fred, Grant, Adam, Bryan, Shawn, Jeremy, Maia, Robert, Nick, and 1 or 2 more. This game got an amazing amount of interference from entities outside our universe. Jeremy lost even though Maia said "his" not once, twice, or thrice, but 6 times! The game ended at about 5:55am. This ending time was fortunate because it allowed a phat 5 minutes of sleep before wake up at 6AM.

Actually, I slept for about 1.5 hours and just made it back in time for closing circle. How wonderful to not be in charge of the rally!

I got home and slept for 8 hours starting at 2:30pm.
