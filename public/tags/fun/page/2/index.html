30 Jun 2016, 17:53

physics lesson

By Rob Nugen

17:53 Thursday 30 June 2016 JST

Physics Girl reminded me of the fun that can be had with magnets.

Today before my (ostensibly English) lesson with GG, I bought a meter of thin, shielded copper wire, a couple magnets, and a little light (emitting diode).

I brought with me a 1.5 volt AA battery and a ferrous, though magnetically neutral Allen wrench hex driver which will act as my electro magnet.

I asked M if she can bring scissors, because the wire needs to be cut and stripped on the ends.

I hope it all works! At the very least, we should be able to light the light by moving the magnets down the wire.

However, I’m not sure if the battery will be strong (charged) enough to make a fun electromagnet.

afterward

Yup, I got schooled. The electromagnet worked, but the magnet-along-wire did not work! GG still enjoyed the lesson, but it’s back to books next week, with 2 new students joining the class!
