skate drum corps

By Rob Nugen

Skate and skate

24 July 1998

Today I went skating!! I totally was all like "I'm going skating" and I just decided to skate to Herrman Park and went basically down Montrose, a pretty heavily trafficked (sp??) 4 lane + turn lane street. The traffic was a bit heavy as it was rush hour, so I mostly stayed on the rough and tumble sidewalks.

The wind was pretty amazingly blowing against me; I looked forward to having it behind me on the way back! I got to the park in about 20 minutes and I was hot!

I met two skaters there and we goofed around the park a a bit, as a group pushing each other a bit more than we would have skated alone.. and we had a great time.

I never learned their names; only that they've been in the Houston area for a year and he's from Australia and I don't know where she's from.

We skated down the hill that is the ampitheater seating for Miller Outdoor Theater and jumped up a wall thing and over the little park railroad tracks and we skated around the lake and just had a fun time.

Then to Rice University we skated and I said "bye" to them at their car and *bing* I realized there was a drum corps competition going on!!

Wow!! So I skated over to where about 100 piece brass was practicing and rested and watched them perform. They practiced until they had ten minutes till they had to march into the stadium.

I watched several of the different groups practice I spent most of my time watching several color guards and they all had sabres!! wow. I loved watching and really longed to be back in the rifle corps from my high school days.

Before heading back home, I got some water from a woman selling programs. I saved the cup she gave me so I could fill it on the way back toward home.

On the way out I watched some drummers march and drum... I love watching the quads drum in perfect syncopation (even if I can't spell it).

Refilled my cup and headed back. The wind had essentially stopped so I didn't get the push I had hoped for, but the going was much easier. I enjoyed that I was skating over crazy bumpy sidewalks and up and down curbs and around cars at intersections.. all while holding my glass of water.

The coolest moment was when I was nearly home.
A truck had just pulled up to the street along which I was skating and waited for traffic. I confirmed that he saw me, so I knew I could safely skate in front of him across the street.

Just as I got near the curb, both skates hit a bump and I essentially bounced over it and off the curb and landed perfectly upright in front of the truck still with water in my cup. That was cool.

By Rob Nugen

Skate and IMax

11 July 1998

Each day this week, I've been working at the New Warrior Lodge. (see Men In Mission for details) The biggest thing we did this week was mail out a couple hundred directories of men who have completed the training. The coolest thing about the work is I get a hug each time I come in to the "office" and I don't have to wear shoes.

Today after working at the Lodge, I did email and caught up with a lot of messages and updated the two youth group websites that I maintain, and actually updated the front page of a third youth website.

I was stir crazy (whatever that means) and decided to go skating. I skated to the bank and deposited a check and went to Walgreen's and got some solution for my contacts. That took only 10 minutes and I wanted to skate more.

So I dropped of the contact solution at my apartment and went back to the intersection of Taft and Allen Parkway.

It's been totally hot as hell... like around 100 degrees, so I was sweatin' like crazy just getting near the intersection.

Panting, I watched the traffic light down the hill from me. I counted off the 60 seconds until it was time for me to race down the hill to the intersection. I got to 40 and the light turned green... waitasecond! I was expecting it to not change yet..

So I stopped and recalibrated my timer.. I needed to count only 30 seconds. Okay, fine.

I skated back to the fire hydrant which is my starting marker. The traffic moved, indicating when I started my mental timer. 1...2...3...4... all the way up to 30 and 

go go go go go go go

down the hill and the light turned green perfectly on time and zoooom across the intersection to the first median and I paused there. None of this instant across the street .. wait it's a big enough gap, so I zipped across the last 4 lanes and to the safety of the sidewalk. Too easy.

"Too easy!" I yelled. "Too too easy."

It was not so easy getting back across.

Like a caged beast, I paced up and down the sidewalk, looking for a break in the rush hour traffic. Up the sidewalk.. nothing but cars whizzing by at 50 to 60 miles per hour.

Down the sidewalk and nothing but cars whizzing by at 50 to 60 miles per hour.. It was the ultimate game of Frogger.

and then I saw it, a break in the traffic and I skated off the curb into the first lane and then zip a car changed lanes, closing off my chosen path. Back onto to the curb I stepped.

Back up the sidewalk, looking for my moment to cross.

Back down, and finally on the back up again pass, I found it and zipped across to the median. Yeah.

From the median I zipped across the remainder of the street and up to my starting point again. Well maybe not zipped... I was plenty hot and just ambled my way up.

I had to do it again.. that first time had just been too easy.

From the fire hydrant I counted off 30 seconds. Many cars and a two pickup trucks were now in line at my light. I reached 30 seconds and began my race down the hill.

Down between the two lanes of cars I cruised, hoping that they wouldn't squish me as they got to move.. I changed my concern, though, as the light seemed to take spectacularly long to turn green, so I hoped simply the light would turn green for me.

Just at the moment of abort, green and I zipped into the intersection before any of the cars began to move. Aww yeah.

To the median I sped and stopped there.. didn't even try to go all the way across. I had to get back home for a shower before meeting Wende, Maggie and John for dinner at La Jaliscience.

I ambled across the street and up the hill even more slowly and was so totally sauna-style hot and sweaty by the time I got back to my apartment..

Took a shower, starting with pretty warm water so as to not send my body into shock.. slowly cooled it down till the hot water was totally off and I was basking in only cold water.

Out of the shower and I was still hot, but had recovered by the time Wende and I got to the restaurant.

(it's 11:30pm and I'm about to go to bed so I'll be quick)

John couldn't make it, so we called Eric and he said he would join us and then John showed up and then Eric showed up so all five of us ate and chilled and John decided to not join us for the IMax movie.

I rode with Maggie in her new red Ford Mustang soft top convertible and we chilled at the museum until the movie.

AMAZON was the IMax movie, and the images were incredible, reminding me how much I really wanna go there.. especially seeing a railroad track wind around the sides of mountains!! Totally wild. The storyline they added was pretty hoaky, but oh well.

Movie ended and we came home and here I am.

I've decided to sleep outside on the top of the back staircase of my apartment building.. just because I can.

good night.

skate with diane

By Rob Nugen

Skating

14 May 1998

I just got back from skating downtown with Diane. It was the first time we've skated in months! And before that it was like half a year before we had skated!

I excitedly described the hopes and visions I have for the cluster, and that I'd like to create a position where I can actually make some dollars doing that which I love.

Diane asked about the new phone number for Wende that's indicated on my answering machine. I explained that she moved out and the conversation turned to how Wende feels about me making dollars as a youth advisor. After hearing the situation, Diane quickly articulated how I could sell the idea of creating a paid position for Wende as well!

I had never thought of such a possibility of having two paid positions because I can hardly fathom me getting paid for it. Wow.

We first skated to Jones Plaza where I noticed there were surprisingly few skaters! When we got up there, we were asked to sign an IpromisenottosueifIbustmyass waiver before we go in.

Diane was pretty entertained reading the legal jargon. However, we were not sufficiently entertained to sign it.

Down the street to a parking lot we went where I got a call from Ms Wende on my cell phone. I described to her the idea and I think she liked it. We'll have to talk about it later.

Wende was entirely pissed and sad that I was out skating with Diane. We spent nearly 10 minutes on the phone while Diane skated in big circles around the parking lot.

Once I got off the phone, Diane was all, "well, did you tell her that we are just friends and not interested in a sexual relationship?" No I hadn't.

Diane encouraged me to call her back, so I did and that helped a lot. Thank you, Diane.

Then we cruised out to our parking lot at the base of the Main Street hill. (That's the hill by University of Houston Downtown campus, quickly down which I love to skate.)

We spent probably 30 minutes there, skating in large circles around the parking lot, with me jumping up and down a pair of sequential curb things. (Yeah that makes sense) Anyway, it was like two curbs right in a row, so I jumped up then down then back up in a tiny little space. Pretty fun stuff.

After a while, we headed back, but stopped by Jones Plaza for me to go car surfing. Well, Diane stopped and I went up the street to catch the next green light pod of traffic. Just as I arrived, the light was fully green and the cars were zipping through so I made a tight turn and joined their ranks.

push push push push until I was up to speed and just behind a long Oldsmobile looking car. I was slightly to his right so he could turn left in front of me with no problem.

Zing through the light and he didn't turn left but he's slowing down.... So I look to the right (it's clear) and slide right into the next lane of traffic and pass the Olds. whhiishh!

There's a car fully slowed in front of me so I slide back left in front of the Olds and zip through the light. Perfect! Push push push push through and keep angling left toward the curb and hop onto the sidewalk to stop.

I love doing that!

Yay! That was super super fun.
