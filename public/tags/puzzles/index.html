Toy store has new puzzles

By Rob Nugen

18:34 Tuesday 06 October 2015 JST

The best new puzzle was a wooden chain like a bicycle chain except some of its links were fixed, as if they were rusty and wouldn’t rotate, except they were actually uncut pieces of wood. Apparently, it’s possible to arrange the chain on a tree shaped board so that all the links exactly cover the tree, including one at the bottom for the trunk. There’s a link featuring a star which seems to fix that point at the top. I wasn’t able to solve it this evening, but I’ll do it before it goes out of season!

Tweaked the Zebra puzzle

By Rob Nugen

17:35 Sunday 23 August 2015 JST

The Zebra Puzzle features brand names of unhealthy products, so I changed them into less-unhealthy food items. I haven’t tried to solve it yet, but here’s my tweaked version:

  1. There are five houses, nationalities, pets, favorite foods, and favorite drinks:
  2. The Englishman lives in the red house.
  3. The Spaniard owns the dog.
  4. Coffee is drunk in the green house.
  5. The Ukrainian drinks tea.
  6. The green house is immediately to the right of the ivory house.
  7. The burrito lover owns snails.
  8. Hamburgers are preferred in the yellow house.
  9. Milk is drunk in the middle house.
  10. The Norwegian lives in the first house.
  11. The man who loves dessert lives in the house next to the man with the fox.
  12. Hamburgers are favorite in the house next to the house where the horse is kept.
  13. The salad lover drinks orange juice.
  14. The Japanese loves raw fish.
  15. The Norwegian lives next to the blue house.

Now, who drinks water? Who owns the zebra?
