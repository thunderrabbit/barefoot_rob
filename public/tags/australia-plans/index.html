13 Jul 1999, 00:00

Australia- Finances

By Rob Nugen

##13 July 1999

It’s four months until my trip. I’ll be out of town two weeks of those four months, fortunately, all in one spot, pretty soon from now. (end of July to 6 August)

I need about 6000 dollars for the trip. Plus Scuba Certification. Am I worried? A little. Do I have a job? Not right now. How’s business? Okay, but not crazy cashflow.

Hmmmm. I’d hate to take out a loan from my dad or a friend, and especially not from a credit card… But I really really want to go.

I’m busy with a couple of different projects that are not currently bringing in any cash. Smallest is KTRU, only 3 hours per week, but in the middle of Tuesday. Then my roller coaster pictures that I’d like to enter into some art shows. If I focus on that (pun intended) it shouldn’t take too long to do. But it will cost on the order of $100. Then my roller coaster book, but I can put that on hold essentially. And last is youth group stuff, which will take the two weeks. After that I can focus on work work work.

I invite the perfect job to come to me starting August 7th.

24 May 1999, 11:00

Tony Synchrony

By Rob Nugen

(transcribed from my diary)

11am Sunday 24 May 1999 CST

Crazy!

I just finished doing laundry and thought, “I’m hungry.” My inner voice pointed toward Kroger. “Go eat.”

So I’m sittin’ in Kroger, eating eggs and biscuits and contemplating life.

Suddenly I see Tony, one of my warrior brothers. Recognition smiles across our faces. “Hey!”

He’s all, “what are you up to?”

“I’m just sittin’ in Kroger, eating eggs and biscuits and contemplating life. In general I’m working saving money to go to Australia in November.”

“You’re kidding.”

“No; I’m very excited about it!”

Turns out Tony lived in Australia for 5 years, and still has contacts there who he said would be happy to give Jason and I a place to stay!! WOW!!

Great stories he told of racing yachts and of Sydney and beaches and the whole fun lifestyle the Aussies live. We only talked for like 10 minutes, but I gave him my card and he’ll get me in touch with his homies. So awesome.

Thank you Angels.

13:29 Wednesday 28 September 2016 JST

17 Apr 1999, 00:00

Jason invited me to OZ

By Rob Nugen

(transcribed from my diary)

##17 April 1999

Jason called this morning. He was all, “Rob, wanna go to Australia with me for four months?”

whoah! said my brain. “Whoah. Four months?”

“Yeah. My mom said that I should travel before I try to do anything else so I told her ‘okay; me and Rob are going to Australia.’”

“Wow. That would be totally great, and I can see the value in it. I totally encourage you to do it. I just couldn’t take that much time off.”

“That’s cool. I just chose you because you’re the only person who’s adult enough to be able to go, but still cool enough not to have a job to keep you here. Plus you’re like the only person I know who I wouldn’t want to kill after being with you for more than 2 days straight.”

Wow. “Wow. I really appreciate that. But I just couldn’t do it…”

All day the conversation stuck in my mind. While en route to Dallas (to visit my brother and Six Flags over Texas) I had time to meditate on it.

Why not go to Australia? I dunno.
Why not go for a shorter time? I dunno.
When could you go? After San Antonio Rally, before New Years.
So that’s all of November and December. yup.
Well?

So I called Jason from the road and left a message. “Yo Jason. I’ve been thinking a lot about this crazy Australia thing. I’ll go with you for a month, and if you want to stay longer, you certainly can.”

Jason called back later and was all, “yeah. I knew you’d do it.”
