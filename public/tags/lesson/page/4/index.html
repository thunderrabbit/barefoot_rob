SS lesson

By Rob Nugen

12:07 Tuesday 01 March 2016 JST

At SS lesson, we described a picture, and practiced asking questions based on the descriptions. We then played a game where teams created questions for a new picture and then took turns asking as the other team answered (without looking at the picture).

That took most of the time. Focus was on correctly using is/are appropriately, and use of the phrase “at least”

In the remaining ten minutes, we went through “Grade 3 Sentence Completion 1” worksheet from this page https://www.englishforeveryone.org/Topics/Sentence-Completion.htm

21 Jan 2016, 20:44

pretty great GG lesson

By Rob Nugen

20:44 Thursday 21 January 2016 JST

I’m enjoying my recent lessons a lot more by being more focused on having the students speak instead of correcting their errors. I pick out a few points perhaps, but basically allow them to chatter away as long as I can essentially understand what they mean.

Today with GG, I did the same exercise as I did down in Fukuura: gave instructions for drawing some lines, circles and dots.. The instructions aren’t perfect in that they won’t result in identical drawings in all correct interpretations, but they give some nice listening practice as well as a chance to use “horizontal” and “vertical” in a somewhat tangible way,.. and they allow me to string together some prepositional phrases which may take some practice to understand!

Specifically,

... in the center of the right side

which M thought she might interpret in a couple of different ways.

On our way back, we talked about Lin and my trip to the US. M asked if I had finished packing. I invited her to ask me if I had started packing!

She asked how long the flight would last: “negative 5 hours” and we talked about jet lag a bit, which led me to talk about consciousness training at The Monroe Institute, which led to discussion of life between lives and finding our purpose in life.

Anyway, good day!
