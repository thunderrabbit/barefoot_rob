Dream

By Rob Nugen

accident dream

6:19am CDT Wednesday 14 June 2000

Walking along a road, making pretty good speed, Wende and I came up to an accident. The reporters were all on camera talking blah blah blah about the accident and then a reporter saw my name on my shirt and asked, “Rob, how do you feel about this?”

“I’ve lived in this area for a really long time and have never actually witnessed an accident so this is really emotional …”

As I spoke, I was watching the workers with a big yellow truck thing dig dirt and stuff out, when a something slipped, and like a hamburger in a bun, a man got caught between two of the huge wheels.

Just at that moment, I cut myself off, “oh my god look over there a guy just go squished I am not making this up!”

The cameras whirled around, as we watched the crane lift the big tire off the guy, and then lift the yellow truck thing that had slipped.

I became all emotional on camera, partially truthfully, partially melodramatically for the camera. I began to cry, “It’s just so sad to have these people die, but I just have to trust that it’s God’s Will and that ultimately there is a purpose to it all.”

Some workers were building a wall to help keep traffic from flying off the bridge. I yelled angrily (for the camera), “I’m glad they’re finally building a fucking wall!” Then quietly to myself, they’ll have to beep that out heh heh.

Wende and I walked away from the cameras just in time to see two duallies (pickup trucks with 2 tires on each side in the back) racing through the mud and then up and onto the roadway. “It’s exactly that kind of driving that caused the accident in the first place,” I complained to Wende.

Dream

7:06am CDT Monday 12 June 2000

Have you ever had a dream that you were naked in public? I just dreamt that Wende was naked in public.

Maggie, Wende and I were all waiting for the school bus. We weren’t high school age, but we were attending high school. While we waited for the bus, Wende was wearing no shirt nor bra.

When the bus arrived, I was all, “here; wear my shirt.” But she wasn’t interested. She just walked very closely behind me, using the back of my shirt to cover the front of her self.

Maggie sat in one of the middle seats, making new friends talking to people. As I stepped on the bus, I found a shirt which I gave to Wende to wear. While waiting for her to put it on, I found a seating chart that the bus driver was writing. She was simply writing for the semester that we sit in the same seats that we chose today.

I wrote Wende and my name in a seat across from Maggie’s.

Though we were in the city, the bus ride was way long; we ended up riding around for like 45 minutes until we were out in the country somewhere at a magnet school. I woke up before we got to school.

dream

By Rob Nugen

Dream

7:10am CST Tuesday 28 March 2000

(transcribed from a dream)

This morning I woke up from a full and complete dream:

Jon Benet Ramsey was being questioned about her parents and their actions and about her actions and just various questions about the living situation in general.

I just happened to be there at the questioning; the meeting was at my church, with a few impartial members asking her things so they could get a better idea about what would be the best course of action. I happened to be there and overheard the whole discussion.

In my men’s group, I’ve been informally trained as a good listener and feedback provider. We each can listen to someone’s story, pick out nuggets or otherwise bring a different light or angle on the situation. I did the same thing in my dream with Jon Benet’s story.

The adults in the meeting began talking amongst themselves, and I got the opportunity to talk with Jon Benet. I told her what I saw from my perspective, and described the best suggestion I had to help her heal the whole thing: forgive those who have hurt her, and mentally bless and love the whole situation.

Please note that I have not once really even listened to or read the whole story as presented by the media. I am not even sure if I’m spelling her name correctly. Back to the dream.

We both had a good cry and I agreed that it could be a difficult thing to forgive, but I believe it’s the only way to heal this wound.

Our conversation ran its entire course naturally with no interruptions. We hugged and said goodbye.

I woke up and recounted the dream. Then I realized, “whoah! wasn’t she murdered?” I wonder if someway somehow our spirits actually had a conversation that manifested itself to me as a dream?
