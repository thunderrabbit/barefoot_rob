impossible puzzle?

By Rob Nugen

18:39 Tuesday 18 August 2015 JST

At the toy store I found a take-apart puzzle featuring two loosely linked nails bent into trianglish G shapes. They are loosely linked, but they’re hard as nails, and I cannot seem to get them separated. Seriously, it looks like they’ve been mis-created (with automation, is that even possible??) or somehow “forced” into an unsolvable state. But they are so hard, I can’t seem to force them back.

I took a picture of a solvable version and an unsolvable version, so get ready to see those images online within the next 1000 years when I think about it, have time, and have internet access to post them.

Break a take-a-part puzzle

By Rob Nugen

##18:42 Tuesday 20 January 2015 JST##

I went to the toy store today and they presented me with a puzzle they had been unable to take apart. It was like a “secret box” puzzle, but was not hollow inside. I set my timer for 30 minutes to make sure I left in time for Mutsumi’s lesson, then went to work.

I was able to solve it in about 25 minutes.

The guy saw that I had done it and came over to investigate; I showed him how it went together. While doing so, I dropped one of the pieces and it came unglued! D’oh!! That’s gonna be hard to put back together so precisely, but they (of course) said it was no problem.

I considered buying it (out of guilt) because I had planned to buy it if I hadn’t been able to solve it before having to go to the lesson..

But I didn’t buy it, nor did I take a picture of it (for Fred: hey, here’s what I didn’t buy for you).

Toy shop near M and M lesson

By Rob Nugen

##18:40 Tuesday 25 November 2014 JST##

I went to the toy shop near the Lotus dealership near Harajuku station. しばたさん recognized me, and I was all お待たせしました [sorry to keep you waiting] when I came in.

They have a cool set of blocks to make marble tracks, including some big ones with near-baseball sized wooden balls that roll down the paths.

I showed her a video of marble track 2, but certainly didn’t make her sit through the whole thing.

She offered me a seat, though, when I got stuck trying to put together a take-apart puzzle. Basically two rings bent in two directions until they were horseshoe shaped. It took me far too long to figure it out; I was mm away from the solution, but hadn’t wanted to squeeze them, which is what was required to solve it.

Next time I go in, I’ll solve the next take-apart puzzle: two giant nails bent and interlinked.
